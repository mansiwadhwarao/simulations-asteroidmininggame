Day 5 Mathematics: Expected Value & Resource Optimization

1. Expected Value (EV) Contribution in Resource Gathering

In game design, drop rates and item values determine how much wealth a player collects on average over time. To calculate the Expected Winnings per Dig, we sum the mathematical expectations of all outcomes:

Gross Expected Value Formula: Gross EV = ∑ (Probability_i × Value_i)

For our default game parameters:

  • Iron: 40% × $50 = $20
  • Copper: 40% × $50 = $20
  • Gold: 10% × $200 = $20
  • Amethyst: 6% × $300 = $18
  • Ruby: 4% × $500 = $20
  • Total Average per Dig (Gross EV): $20 + $20 + $20 + $18 + $20 = $98.00

2. Holding vs. Selling: Compounding Appreciation

When you mine a rare resource (Gold, Amethyst, Ruby), you have a strategic choice:

💵 Sell Immediately

Guarantees liquidity. You get cash instantly to purchase high-value upgrades like the **Excavator** or **Fuel** to run it.

📈 Hold Ores in Inventory

Gold, Amethyst, and Ruby values increase by 10%, 20%, and 30% of their base values every 10 seconds. In 30 seconds, a Ruby goes from $500 to $950! This represents the **Time Value of Money** and opportunity cost.

💡 Optimal Strategy: Hold rare items until they appreciate to maximum values, but don't hold them so long that you restrict your ability to buy speed upgrades! Speed (digs per second) is a multiplier on your wealth generation rate.

3. Probability Manipulation: The Metal Detector

Equipping the **Metal Detector** triples the probabilities of finding Gold (10% → 30%), Amethyst (6% → 18%), and Ruby (4% → 12%). This raises the probability of rare drops from 20% to 60%! How does this change our Expected Value? The remaining 40% probability is split between Iron and Copper (20% each).

New Expected Value calculation with Metal Detector: EV_new = (20% × $50) + (20% × $50) + (30% × $200) + (18% × $300) + (12% × $500) EV_new = $10 + $10 + $60 + $54 + $60 = $194.00 per dig!

Equipping the Metal Detector nearly **doubles** your expected winnings per dig from $98.00 to $194.00. This shows the massive power of mathematical leverage!
